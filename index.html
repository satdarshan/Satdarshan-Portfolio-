Dear Hiring Team,

I am excited to apply for a Registered Dental Hygienist position at your practice. As a new graduate from Shoreline Community College's Bachelor of Science in Dental Hygiene program, I bring hands-on clinical experience across a range of settings and a genuine passion for helping patients achieve lasting oral and systemic health.

Throughout my clinical training, I have had the privilege of working with diverse patient populations — from pediatric patients at the UW Center for Pediatric Dentistry to adults with complex needs at a Community Health Center in Everett and at the University of Washington Dental Hygiene Clinic. In these roles, I performed preventive prophylaxis, administered local anesthesia, applied Silver Diamine Fluoride, implemented nonsurgical periodontal therapies, and provided tailored oral health instruction.

I also bring prior experience as a Dental Hygiene Assistant at Harbor Square Dental, where I developed a strong foundation in restorative support and patient care alongside a collaborative team.

My additional background in dementia and mental health care, early childhood education, and nursing assisting allows me to connect with patients of all ages and comfort levels with empathy and ease.

I am seeking a position where I can grow as a clinician, contribute meaningfully to a team, and build long-term relationships with patients. Thank you for your time and consideration — I look forward to the opportunity to connect.

My Philosophy & Goals

Clinical Philosophy

I believe that dental hygiene is more than a clinical procedure — it's a relationship. My approach begins with listening: understanding a patient's fears, history, and goals before I ever pick up an instrument. I aim to create an environment where patients feel genuinely comfortable and empowered to take ownership of their oral health.

My background working with pediatric patients, elderly populations, and individuals with special needs has taught me that effective care is deeply personalized. Every patient walks in with a unique story, and I tailor my communication and treatment to meet them where they are.

Professional Goals

  • Obtain my Washington State RDH license and establish myself in a patient-centered practice that values preventive care
  • Continue developing my skills in periodontal therapy, local anesthesia administration, and caries prevention
  • Build long-term relationships with patients and contribute to a collaborative, supportive team environment
  • Engage in community oral health outreach, particularly for underserved and pediatric populations
  • Pursue continuing education to stay current with evolving evidence-based dental hygiene practices
