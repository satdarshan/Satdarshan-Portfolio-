My Philosophy and Goals

Clinical Philosophy

I believe that dental hygiene is more than a clinical procedure; it is a relationship. My approach begins with listening: understanding a patient's fears, history, and goals before I ever pick up an instrument. I aim to create an environment where patients feel genuinely comfortable and empowered to take ownership of their oral health.

A core part of my practice is personalized oral hygiene instruction. I work with each patient to build a home care game plan that fits their lifestyle, supports their oral biome, and gives them the tools they need to achieve a healthier baseline between visits.

My background working with pediatric patients, elderly populations, and individuals with special needs has taught me that effective care is deeply personalized. Every patient walks in with a unique story, and I tailor my communication and treatment to meet them where they are.

3-Quarter Clinical Case Study

From Severe Unstable Perio to Stable: A Full Patient Journey

This comprehensive case study spans three academic quarters and documents a patient who had not received dental care in over 8 years. Upon initial assessment, the patient presented with severe white coat syndrome (dental anxiety) and severe unstable periodontal disease. Building trust was as important as the clinical work. Through consistent, patient-centered appointments, the relationship was developed slowly and intentionally.

Clinically, treatment included full mouth radiographic series, comprehensive periodontal charting, scaling and root planing, and ongoing re-evaluation. Personalized oral hygiene instruction and home care plans were crafted at each visit to progressively build a routine that fit the patient's lifestyle. By the final quarter, the patient had achieved stable periodontal status and a consistent, manageable home care routine, a meaningful transformation in both oral health and confidence in the dental setting.

Working closely with this patient went well beyond the operatory. Between appointments, the focus was on building sustainable daily habits through genuine collaboration. A personalized nutritional plan was developed together, targeting a reduction in fermentable carbohydrate frequency to lower acid attack exposure, increasing fluoride intake through optimized toothpaste selection and supplemental rinses, and improving hydration habits to support healthy salivary flow. This day-to-day coaching approach was central to the patient's overall progress and helped translate clinical improvements into lasting lifestyle changes they could realistically maintain.

Community Oral Health Rotation

Senior Living Oral Health Education Program

Developed and delivered a comprehensive oral health education program for residents of a senior living facility. The curriculum focused on oral cancer awareness, teaching residents what to look for, how to distinguish normal tissues from changes that warrant professional evaluation, and why early detection matters.

The program also covered proper care for prostheses, implants, bridges, and partials, addressing the unique maintenance needs of each. Residents received practical guidance on combating harmful oral bacteria, reducing plaque and biofilm accumulation, and maintaining the health of their natural teeth and surrounding tissues. Pre and post surveys were used to assess knowledge outcomes and evaluate program effectiveness, ensuring the education was both accessible and impactful for this population.

Caries Control and Prevention

High-Risk to Manageable: ADA Caries Risk Reduction Case

This case study documents a patient who initially presented as high caries risk according to the ADA Caries Risk Assessment checklist. Over multiple appointments, a comprehensive and individualized prevention plan was developed and refined collaboratively with the patient.

The approach addressed multiple contributing factors including dietary habits, home care routine development, fluoride use, and patient education on caries etiology and the role of the oral microbiome. Current restorative needs were identified and coordinated as part of the overall plan. Through consistent re-evaluation, motivational interviewing, and evidence-based preventive strategies, the patient progressed from high caries risk to a manageable moderate-to-low risk level, demonstrating meaningful and measurable improvement in their long-term oral health trajectory.

This work extended into the patient's daily life. A personalized nutritional plan was developed collaboratively to address the frequency of fermentable carbohydrates and sugar-containing beverages that were driving repeated acid attacks and demineralization cycles. Fluoride intake was strategically increased through prescription-strength fluoride products, optimized toothpaste selection, and fluoride rinse protocols tailored to the patient's routine. Meal timing, snacking patterns, saliva-supporting habits, and hydration were all discussed and adjusted over time. This close, day-to-day collaboration with the patient was essential to achieving the measurable risk reduction reflected on the ADA Caries Risk Assessment checklist.
